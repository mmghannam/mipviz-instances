# nursescheduling

The `nursesched-*` MIPLIB instances come from Santos, Toffolo, Gomes,
and Ribas (2016)[^1], whose paper describes integer-programming
techniques for the **nurse rostering problem** — the problem of
assigning nurses to shifts over a planning horizon subject to labour
and preference constraints. The paper title ("Integer programming
techniques for the nurse rostering problem") is the basis on which the
instances were published.

The nurse rostering problem is also the subject of periodic
international competitions, so the MIPLIB instances sit inside a
larger community benchmark ecosystem[^2].

## Further reading

Papers from the OpenAlex citation graph that cite the origin and stay
within nurse rostering / nurse scheduling:

- **The Second International Nurse Rostering Competition** — Ceschia,
  Dang, De Causmaecker et al. (2018)[^2]. A community benchmark
  alongside the MIPLIB instances.
- **A survey of nurse rostering solution methodologies** — Ngoo, Goh,
  Sze et al. (2022)[^3] reviews methods published between 2012 and
  2021.
- **A hybrid integer programming and constraint programming
  approach** — Rahimian, Akartunalı, Levine (2017)[^4].
- **Fix-and-optimize with simulated annealing** — Turhan, Bilgen
  (2020)[^5].
- **A two-phase adaptive variable neighbourhood approach** —
  Tassopoulos, Solos, Beligiannis (2015)[^6].
