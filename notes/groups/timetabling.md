# timetabling

The `timetabling` MIPLIB instances come from Fonseca, Santos, Carrano,
and Stidsen (2017)[^1], which proposes **integer programming techniques
for educational timetabling** — assigning courses, teachers, rooms,
and time slots under institutional rules and soft preferences.


## Further reading

Papers from the OpenAlex citation graph that cite the origin and stay
within educational timetabling:

- **Educational Timetabling: Problems, Benchmarks, and State-of-the-Art
  Results** — Ceschia, Di Gaspero, Schaerf (2022)[^2]. A recent survey
  of the six "standard" timetabling formulations and their benchmark
  instances.
- **A survey of optimisation methodologies in school timetabling** —
  Tan, Goh, Kendall et al. (2020)[^3].
- **Local search algorithms for high school timetabling** — Saviniec
  & Constantino (2017)[^4].
- **Pattern-based models and cooperative parallel metaheuristic for
  high school timetabling** — Saviniec, Santos, Costa et al. (2019)[^5].
- **The multiphase course timetabling problem** — Esmaeilbeigi,
  Mak-Hau, Yearwood et al. (2021)[^6].
